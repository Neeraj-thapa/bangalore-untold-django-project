Historic Places

Devanahalli Fort

Description: Located 35 km north of Bangalore, Devanahalli Fort is a historic fortress that dates back to 1501. It is the birthplace of Tipu Sultan, the renowned "Tiger of Mysore." The fort showcases impressive architecture with semi-circular bastions and is a testament to the region's rich history.

Makali Durga

Description: Situated about 60 km from Bangalore, Makali Durga is a hill fort perched at an elevation of 1,117 meters. It's a popular trekking destination, offering panoramic views of the surrounding landscape and a serene environment for nature enthusiasts.

Bangalore Fort

Description: Originally built by Kempe Gowda in 1537 as a mud fort, Bangalore Fort was later converted into a stone fort by Hyder Ali in the 18th century. Located in the heart of the city, it stands as a reminder of Bangalore's historical significance and architectural heritage.

Gudibanda Fort

Description: Approximately 90 km from Bangalore, Gudibanda Fort is a lesser-known gem featuring a seven-story fortification. Built in the 17th century, it offers insight into ancient military architecture and provides trekkers with a rewarding climb and scenic views.

Turahalli Forest

Description: Located on the outskirts of Bangalore, Turahalli Forest is the city's last surviving forest area. It's a popular spot for rock climbing, cycling, and nature walks, offering a quick escape from the urban hustle.

Channarayana Durga

Description: This hill fort, located near Madhugiri, was built in the 17th century and has witnessed numerous battles. It's a favored trekking destination, with ruins that narrate tales of its storied past and offer trekkers a blend of adventure and history.

Huliyurdurga Fort

Description: Located about 80 km from Bangalore, Huliyurdurga is one of the nine hill forts built by Kempe Gowda. The fort sits atop a hill resembling a sleeping tiger and offers trekkers a challenging climb amidst scenic surroundings.

Shivagange Hill Temple

Description: Known as "Dakshina Kashi," Shivagange is both a pilgrimage center and a trekking spot. The hill resembles a shivalinga and has a spring called "Gange," making it a significant spiritual destination.

Bhoga Nandeeshwara Temple

Description: Situated at the base of Nandi Hills, this temple dates back to the 9th century and showcases Dravidian architecture. It's dedicated to Lord Shiva and is one of the oldest temples in Karnataka.

Begur Fort

Description: Begur Fort, located in Begur, Bangalore, is a historical landmark dating back to the 9th and 10th centuries. It played a significant role during the Chola dynasty and offers insights into the region's rich past.
